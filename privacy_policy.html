Privacy Policy for Hockey Pulse

Last updated: 21.09.2023

Hockey Pulse team ("we," "us," or "our") operates the Hockey Pulse mobile application (the "App"). This page informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information and health-related data, such as heart rate information, that we receive from users of the App.

By using the App, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Information Collection and Use

While using our App, we may ask you to provide us with certain personally identifiable information that can be used to contact or identify you. Personally identifiable information may include but is not limited to your name, email address, and profile picture. This information is used to enable you to log in to the App using third-party providers such as Google.

Additionally, with your explicit consent, the App may also collect and store heart rate data from sensors on your mobile device or connected wearable devices. This heart rate data is used to provide features and services within the App that require or utilize this information, including calculating shifts during ice hockey matches and generating related statistics.

Apple HealthKit Data

Hockey Pulse allows you to import health data from Apple HealthKit or Strava. By using this feature, you grant us permission to access and process certain health-related data, including heart rate information, in accordance with the authorization procedures determined by Apple HealthKit or Strava. This data is used to enhance your ice hockey match analysis and shift calculations within the App.

Log Data

We may also collect information that your mobile device sends whenever you use our App ("Log Data"). This Log Data may include information such as your device's Internet Protocol ("IP") address, device name, operating system version, the configuration of the App when utilizing our service, the time and date of your use of the App, and other statistics.

Security

The security of your Personal Information, health data, and other information is important to us, but remember that no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.
